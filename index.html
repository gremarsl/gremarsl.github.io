Marcel Utermann

Senior Embedded Systems Engineer

I am a Senior Software Development Engineer at Vector Informatik GmbH in Stuttgart, Germany. I specialize in designing and implementing safe, high-performance embedded firmware, real-time control algorithms, and automated validation environments for automotive systems.

Currently, I lead a team of developers focused on building remote execution environments and validation frameworks for electronic control units (ECUs). My work covers architectural decisions, C/C++ development for ARM Cortex-M and Aurix Tricore microcontrollers, timing analysis, and continuous runtime behavior profiling across a distributed hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) cloud execution network.

I hold a Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering from the Technical University of Dresden, with a specialization in Systems Engineering and Product Development. My academic and early research work centered on automation technology, evaluating telematics arrays, and constructing mathematical drive models and simulations using Python, LabVIEW, and Modelica.

My engineering philosophy is rooted in the belief that excellent systems solve real problems. I strongly follow the John Gall paradigm: "A complex system that works is invariably found to have evolved from a simple system that worked." Effective, reliable embedded systems are built from clean hardware-level bring-ups and iterative, measured development.

Outside of engineering, I am an active endurance athlete, enjoying running and road cycling, as well as racket sports like tennis and badminton.
